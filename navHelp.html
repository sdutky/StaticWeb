Navigating A Contextual Index


A contextual index gives a reader the means to explore a text and to quickly locate keywords or phrases and view the passages in which they occur. It has a long history. It has been called keyword in context (KWIC) and permuted indices ( see url 👉 en.wikipedia.org 🔗 ). Typically they have been used to index titles, sections, and abstracts. We form a contextual index from the entire text of a document.

Keywords are the fundamental unit of this index. They begin with a letter, contain only a small number of digits and are not found in a list of stopwords. Keywords found in a list of non-stop words are exempt from these restrictions. There are also labels such as {author}, {subhead}, {section}, and {bullet} to organize and index key parts of the text.

A contextual index is structured as the Full Context, the Scrolling Context, the Keyword Index, and the Thumb Index
Please try this live example from the following passage:

“This devoted band called itself the Eldorado Exploring Expedition, and I believe they were sworn to secrecy. Their talk, however, was the talk of sordid buccaneers: it was reckless without hardihood, greedy without audacity, and cruel without courage; there was not an atom of foresight or of serious intention in the whole batch of them, and they did not seem aware these things are wanted for the work of the world. To tear treasure out of the bowels of the land was their desire, with no more moral purpose at the back of it than there is in burglars breaking into a safe. Who paid the expenses of the noble enterprise I don’t know; but the uncle of our manager was leader of that lot.”
Joseph Conrad, The Heart of Darkness www.gutenberg.org https://www.gutenberg.org/cache/epub/219/pg219-images.html

These are constructed by sliding a fixed window across each passage. Each position of the window corresponds to a line in the Scrolling Context. The keyword in the middle of the window becomes the boldface primary keyword of that line. Clicking on it goes to its corresponding position in the passage. The triangle ▲ on its left links to its location in the Keyword Index. The keywords to its left and right link to the lines in the Scrolling Context where each appears as the primary keyword.

The number of keywords in the Thumb Index varies with the total number of keywords. The first keyword on each page of the Keyword Index always appears in the collated grid of the Thumb Index. Every page in the Contextual Index has a link in its header to the top of Thumb Index. The headers in the pages of the Keyword Index go directly to the position of their first keyword.
